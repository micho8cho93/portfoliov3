# Transport Layer

The transport layer decides how data chunks move between endpoints: carefully confirmed or quickly sent.

<InternetLayerVisual layer="transport" analogyLabel="Registered mail lane vs postcard lane." />

## Real-World Analogy

Think of two delivery lanes:

- `TCP` is registered mail: numbered parcels, receipts, and resend when one is missing.
- `UDP` is postcards: fast to send, but no guarantee every card arrives.

## Components In This Layer (From the PDF Definitions)

- `TCP`: Connection-oriented delivery with ordering, reliability, and acknowledgments.
- `UDP`: Connectionless delivery with low overhead and no delivery confirmation.

## What The Loop Visual Shows

The top conveyor lane is TCP with ACK and resend indicators. The lower lane is UDP, where packets burst quickly with no confirmation stage.
